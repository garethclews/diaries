Post of the week

This week has been spent mostly reading mathematics (which I won’t bore you with) so my post of the week is the light summary of OpenAI releasing that they said was too dangerous. As exciting as it is terrifying.

Blogs and commentary

  • Monzo have recently scaled up their data team from 1 to 30 people. Here’s how they did it.
  • Ever wonder whether given housing information you could try to predict airbnb prices? Even if you didn’t we have a post for you.
  • As we have previously seen, it is hard to implement any form of AI to provide benefits in health. Springer’s BSC discusses the key challenges in delivering clinical impact.
  • I read a nice little paper (only 11 pages) providing a language dataset for understanding humour.

Tools of the trade

  • Resampling (not a new topic) is gaining interest as a way to solve machine learning problems for e.g. estimation of variance. Lucky for us, there’s a free book on the topic to get us started. Especially if you are looking to start making use of bootstrapping to build an understanding of causation in your problems.
  • In a bid to become more productive with my time, I’m going to trial the use of the pomodoro technique. Whether or not this suits my style of getting things done I’m not sure yet, but it may work for you.
  • For those looking for an introduction to the mechanics of deep learning, I recommend the deep learning book from MIT press.
